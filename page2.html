Experiences

If you ever consider visiting Tonga you might want to visit these places Tongatapu, ‘Eua and Ha’apai as it is three of the most popular tourist destinations.

Tongatapu

Tongatapu is the main island and the capital of Tonga, it is also the largest and most popular island of Tonga, The island is relatively flat compared to other parts of Tonga, with some areas featuring low-lying coral atolls and sandy beaches. Tongatapu has many places to visit, so stop by the:

Mapu’a Vaea Blowholes

On a particularly good day at Mapu'a 'a Vaea, also known as Chief's Whistles, hundreds of blowholes shoot up into the sky simultaneously. To witness this spectacle, visit the area on a windy day with a strong swell. This is when the surf, pushed up through eroded holes in the coralline limestone, spouts 30 metres high. The rocks dotted with blowholes extend for five kilometres along the south coast, close to the village of Houma. And best of all it’s free!

Anahulu Cave

Anahulu Cave is a fascinating cave system located on Tongatapu Island in Tonga. It is famous for its natural beauty and rich historical significance. The caves are believed to be over 3,000 years old and were formed by the erosion of limestone and volcanic rocks. They are renowned for their stunning limestone formations, crystal-clear pools, and hidden waterfalls. The cave is quite popular among tourists, and guided tours offer an immersive experience by exploring the interconnected chambers and pools while learning about the cave's geology, history, and diverse ecosystem. Visitors can also enjoy swimming in the underground pools and boat rides along the subterranean river. If you're looking to marvel at the wonders of Tonga's natural landscape, then Anahulu Cave is a must-visit destination.

The Talamahu Market

Wander through the aisles at Talamahu, Tonga's main fresh-produce hub. You’ll find produce piled into handmade woven-frond baskets, branches of bananas, colourful pyramids of fruit and a few cooked-food stalls – plus outstanding (and affordable) Tongan arts and crafts. The whole place buzzes with talk and commerce, particularly on Saturday mornings.

'Eua

‘Eua is the oldest island in Tonga and is said to be around 40-million-years-old. The land has formed some amazing and breathtaking features over its ancient history, giving you plenty of natural attractions to explore from volcanic sites, whale sightseeing and hiking to see the beautiful view of the whole island! If you are in Tongatapu make sure to stop by ‘Eua as it’s very close to the island! You’ll get to experience places like:

Tufuvai Beach

Tufuvai Beach in Tonga offers serene isolation with powdery white sands, turquoise waters, and vibrant marine life perfect for snorkelling. It's an unforgettable destination for relaxation and exploration away from crowded tourist spots.

Whale watching

Between July and November, magnificent humpback whales can be seen swimming close to 'Eua Island while on their way to and from breeding grounds around the Kingdom of Tonga. You can watch their powerful aerial displays while relaxing on a cliff top or on the beach. To get a closer look, you can arrange for a whale-watching boat trip. However, please note that swimming with the whales is not allowed.

Scuba diving

The 'Eua Island diving spot is one of the world's best for scuba diving. It has drop-offs, sea mountains, caves, and canyons. At 28 meters deep, you'll find the last hole in the cathedral. It's a natural spectacle that is hard to beat. There's a 20-meter tunnel in the end wall that leads to a big cave, and other tunnels are yet to be explored. You'll see sharks, groupers, school fish, and more, before coming across a drop-off full of coral and fish. School fish will guide you back to the wall, where another cave is waiting for you to explore.

Ha’apai

Koa Island

Kao Island is a remote volcanic island in the Pacific Ocean, north of Tonga. It's the highest peak in Tonga at 1,046 meters with visible volcanic activity. Despite this, it's popular for hiking, mountaineering, and cultural significance. Visitors should exercise caution due to the rugged terrain.

Horseback riding

The horseback riding experience in Ha'apai is an exceptional and unforgettable way to discover the natural beauty of Tonga. With its breathtaking scenery, expertly trained n horses, and knowledgeable guides, visitors are assured to have an experience they will cherish forever. Whether they are seasoned riders or beginners, there is a tour option available for everyone. Therefore, this activity is a must-do for all those visiting Ha'apai.

Tofua Volcano

Tofua Island in the Kingdom of Tonga is a beautiful island with a large crater, rugged landscape, pristine beaches, and an active volcano. It is known for hot springs, black sand beach, and cultural heritage. Captain Bligh and his crew spent time on Tofua Island during the infamous Bounty Mutiny of 1789. The island is located approximately 45 kilometres west of the main island of Tongatapu and covers an area of approximately 13.5 square kilometres.
